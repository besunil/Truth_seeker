Reading

The books I press into people's hands. Not a complete list — a filtered one. Each of these survived a reread.

Thinking

  • Meditations — Marcus Aurelius The operating manual for a calm mind, written by a man with no audience but himself.
  • Thinking, Fast and Slow — Daniel Kahneman You will never trust your first impression again. Correctly.
  • The Beginning of Infinity — David Deutsch The most optimistic serious book ever written. Progress is unbounded if explanations keep improving.
  • Poor Charlie's Almanack — Charlie Munger A lattice of mental models from a man who read everything and said exactly what he thought.

Work & Craft

  • The War of Art — Steven Pressfield Names the enemy — Resistance — and teaches you to show up anyway. Read every year.
  • Deep Work — Cal Newport The case for the undivided hour, which is where everything valuable gets made.
  • Hackers & Painters — Paul Graham Proof that essays can change careers. The title essay alone is worth the price.
  • Several Short Sentences About Writing — Verlyn Klinkenborg Will quietly dismantle everything school taught you about sentences.

Life

  • Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl Meaning is found in the stance you take toward what you cannot change.
  • Walden — Henry David Thoreau An experiment in wanting less, two centuries before it became urgent.
  • The Courage to Be Disliked — Ichiro Kishimi & Fumitake Koga Adlerian freedom as a dialogue. Uncomfortable in the useful way.

Fiction

  • The Brothers Karamazov — Fyodor Dostoevsky Every important argument about faith, doubt, and family, staged as a murder trial.
  • Stoner — John Williams A quiet life rendered so precisely it becomes epic.
  • The Remains of the Day — Kazuo Ishiguro What dignity costs when it comes at the expense of honesty with oneself.

Suggestions welcome — send me the book that changed you.
